Iteration Workshops

Facilitating the Creative Process

Due to my experience teaching the art of creative thinking and project management, I have given multiple talks, notably within the tech industry, as well as engineering and product development fields. Most recently, I facilitated a creativity workshop at Nexient in February 2019. I focused on teaching abstract painting, and the ability to let go of concrete images and explore feelings and ideas through shapes and color. In May 2019, I gave an iteration workshop on the importance of creative thinking in relation to innovation in product development. Both of these recent workshops were hugely successful, as feedback was ecstatic. It was great for developers, designers, and engineers to think of the 'Why', in design, in a tactile way.

Background

How I Came to UX Design

Design has always been my cup of tea. From chairs to clothing, to logos and branding, I have chased design since I built my first lemonade stand. This propelled me to pursue an education in product development at FIDM in Los Angeles, California. After working in the Garment District, multiple wholesalers, advertising firms, and Hugo Boss, I decided to go back to school for more knowledge in the form of a Graphic Design education at Eastern Michigan University. I worked as a package designer and branding specialist, which gave me great understanding in how customers view information. That's when I knew I loved to visualize information. Sketching, wire-framing, user flows, and prototyping, took hold of my studies. At the same time, my interest in the creative thinking process as a means to innovation, took hold. I opened an art and design maker space in downtown Ann Arbor in 2014. I created multiple visual displays, advertising, branding, and conducted many interviews for user personas to decipher motivations for the business. I created two websites and continue to engage with over 40 students that intermittently visit the studio monthly. Which brings me to UX design. The culmination of my skillset gives me a strong background in research, communication, and knowledge in many industries. I was driven to design because I felt excited to learn about people's goals, help them solve problems, and make their lives easier. With my diverse skillset I want to connect, listen, ideate, inspire, revise, succees, try, learn, and design for the current marketplace and future.
