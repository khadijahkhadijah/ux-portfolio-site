Seedling is an application in discovery phase for women over the age of 35 attempting to conceive naturally or through in-vitro fertilization. Thus needing a technical aspect of calendars and recording as well as an emotional support aspect to promote calmness for fertility.

Design has always been been my cup of tea…. Or coffee! From chair design to fashion design to logos, branding, and the different ways one could make a tea kettle, I have been obsessed with making! Since my first lemonade stand that had every single detail thought out with headers, footers, and color schemes, my parents knew I would create a career out of my love for design. This led me to design school at FIDM in Los Angeles California where I focused on an education in Product Design with an emphasis on Merchandise Marketing. This rich education taught me to really focus on building brands, identity and curating a story for each label I worked for including Hugo Boss. I pursued further education at Eastern Michigan University where I studied Graphic Design. I obtained multiple positions since then working for big local brands in advertising, package design, communication design and now to UX design. The natural evolution of my career into UX was evident when I realized how much I loved to understand people and their motivations. I have learned to really listen to people, study them, and hold natural conversations that are actually questionnaires. I like to really understand the who, what and why of UX research. After working for multiple agencies I took time to study human development and psychology in hopes to understand client motivations more. I started with the creative process and how it pertains to innovation. I opened an art and maker space called “MAKE” for families in downtown A2 in 2014. I conducted countless hours of research into understanding how we can inspire people and their natural inclinations. Simultaneously I have held multiple ideation and brainstorming workshops within the tech industry to help loosen up the creative process to make room for this innovation in product development and design. The culmination of my skillset gives me a strong background in not only technical skills but also compelling graphics, communication, psychology, and the ability to conduct research that makes an impact. I want to help make peoples lives easier through good design. With my diverse skillset I want to connect, listen, ideate, inspire, revise, succeed, try, learn, and design for the current marketplace and future!

Therasupport

Web Design

Therasupport Inc. had a core message to send out to TBI (tramautic brain injury) patients/clients. Therasupport’s message was clear; to implement art and skill based training for people suffering from this condition yet still able to resume some life activities. We rebranded the website to have subtle hints of faces behind all pages. Like it or not, these days your success is tied to how your user feels about you and your core message. We used different nuanced design cues to make strong, long lasting relationships with the target audience by taking the time to understand who those users are, how they interact with Therasupports message and product, and how your brand impacted them, and then designing with those criteria in mind. At the end of the day, I Ain to design any product so that it works the way your users expect and it impacts their lives. Therasupport clients felt the artistic touches and compelled to reach out for service with 20% increase in program membership after new design went live.

Wireframes & Sketching

Sketching sets the tone for the rest of the design process. As a graphic, visual and UX designer I think one of the most important steps that is often skipped over is sketching. I have spent many years speaking on the importance of brainstorming. Sitting down and thinking, jotting down ideas, illustrations, and many different bubbles and sprouts upon a page. Sketching allows you to entertain all the possibilities of what your interface, print material or multimedia and what it COULD become. A thoughtful designer, one who aims to approach design with the big picture in mind while at the same time staying close to the client’s thoughts and feelings is a designer that will not only create an impact but also great results.
